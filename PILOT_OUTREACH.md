# Fonda — Pilot Outreach Strategy
## How to sign 3 pilot hotels

---

## 1. Who you're looking for (ICP)

Not every boutique hotel is the right first pilot. You want properties where the problem is acute, the decision-maker is reachable, and the technical setup is straightforward.

**The ideal pilot hotel:**

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| 20–80 rooms | Small enough that the GM handles their own morning prep and email. Large enough that they're genuinely overwhelmed by it. |
| Independent or small group (1–3 properties) | No procurement process, no IT department. The GM or owner says yes and you're in. |
| Uses Apaleo or MEWS | Fonda already integrates with both. Zero custom work needed. |
| Uses Gmail for hotel operations | Your email assistant works on Gmail. Avoids the "we use Outlook" conversation. |
| 4-star or design-forward | These GMs care about quality of output. A beautiful briefing resonates. They're also more likely to be tech-curious. |
| City hotel or urban boutique | Busier daily operations = more pain. Resort-only properties have different rhythms. |

**What disqualifies a property for now:**
- Part of a chain (Marriott, IHG, Accor) — too much bureaucracy
- Uses Opera, Cloudbeds, or other PMS — no integration yet
- Uses Outlook or a shared inbox — Gmail OAuth won't work
- Under 15 rooms — owner probably handles everything themselves without feeling overwhelmed

## 2. Where to find them

### Channel A — Apaleo's own ecosystem (highest quality leads)

Apaleo publishes a marketplace and runs an active community. Hotels listed there are confirmed Apaleo users — the hardest qualification is already done.

- **Apaleo Community** (community.apaleo.com) — active forum where hotel GMs and ops managers post. Find threads about pain points, introduce yourself, engage genuinely before pitching.
- **Apaleo Marketplace partner list** — browse hotels featured as case studies or success stories. These are usually 4-star independents who are tech-forward.
- **LinkedIn: search "Apaleo" + "General Manager"** — returns GMs who publicly mention their PMS.

### Channel B — Design hotel collections

These associations curate exactly the kind of properties you want. Browse their member directories:

- **Design Hotels** (designhotels.com/membership) — member directory is public
- **Small Luxury Hotels of the World** (slh.com) — searchable by city, size, style
- **Mr & Mrs Smith** (mrandmrssmith.com) — boutique-focused, public hotel profiles
- **Tablet Hotels** (tablethotels.com) — independent luxury, searchable

For each property you find: Google "[Hotel Name] General Manager" or check LinkedIn to find who to contact.

### Channel C — LinkedIn direct search

The most scalable channel once you have a working message.

Search string: `"General Manager" "boutique hotel" [city]`

Target cities to start: Barcelona, Madrid, Amsterdam, Lisbon, London, Berlin. These have dense boutique hotel clusters, English-speaking GMs, and strong Apaleo/MEWS adoption.

Filter by: 2nd-degree connections first (warmer intro), then 3rd-degree.

### Channel D — Your existing network

Before you do any cold outreach, ask yourself:
- Do you know anyone who stays frequently at boutique hotels and could make an intro?
- Do you know anyone in hospitality tech, hotel consulting, or F&B who has GM contacts?

One warm intro is worth 20 cold messages. Even a LinkedIn mutual connection dramatically improves response rates.

### Channel E — Instagram

Boutique hotels are obsessive about Instagram. If you can't find a GM's email, the hotel's Instagram DMs often get read by the owner or GM directly. Especially for smaller properties.

Search: boutique hotel hashtags by city (`#boutiquehotelbarcelona`, `#designhotellondon`), find beautifully shot properties under 80 rooms, check their bio for a contact.

## 3. The outreach sequence

Three touches maximum before you move on. Most responses come on touch 1 or 2.

```
Day 1   →  LinkedIn connection request (with note)
Day 4   →  LinkedIn message (the actual ask)
Day 11  →  Email follow-up (if no response)
Day 18  →  Final follow-up (short, close the loop)
```

If no response after day 18: move on. Don't follow up a 5th time — it damages your reputation in a small industry where everyone talks.

## 5. The demo / first call

When a GM says yes to a call, the goal is not to sell — it's to make them feel smart for agreeing.

**20-minute structure:**

| Minutes | What you do |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | Ask about their morning routine. "What does your first hour look like? Where do you spend the most time before the team arrives?" Let them describe the pain before you show the solution. |
| 3–12 | Screen share. Show the dashboard. Walk through a sample briefing — read some of it aloud. Show the email inbox with a draft ready. Show "Ask Fonda" answering a question about arrivals. Don't narrate features — narrate the day. "So imagine it's 6:45am. You open this…" |
| 12–17 | "Does this match the kind of thing that would save you time?" Then stop talking. |
| 17–20 | If yes: "The next step is connecting your Apaleo account — takes about 10 minutes and I'll do it with you on a second call. Want to book that now?" |

**The critical thing:** never say "AI" during the demo. Say "Fonda pulls your data and writes this." The output speaks for itself. Saying "AI" makes them think of ChatGPT and raises their guard.

## 6. Objections and how to handle them

**"We're very busy right now / it's peak season."**
> "Completely understand — that's exactly why I wanted to reach out now. The briefing saves most time during busy periods when you have 90% occupancy and things move fast. Happy to do the setup in a single 30-minute call that fits around you."

**"We're already using [some other tool] for reporting."**
> "That makes sense — Fonda isn't a reporting tool, it's more like a daily digest that reads your reports for you and surfaces what matters. Most GMs use both. Would you be open to seeing how they compare?"

**"I'd need to check with our owner / IT / headquarters."**
> "Of course. If it helps, I can send you a one-page summary you could share with them — it explains what Fonda accesses, what it doesn't touch, and how the Apaleo connection works." Then send the one-pager (see below) and follow up in 5 days.

**"Is my data safe?"**
> "Fonda reads from Apaleo via their official API — the same way your channel manager connects. We don't store reservation data, we use it to generate the briefing and then discard it. I'm happy to walk through exactly what permissions we request if that's useful."

**"What does it cost?"**
> "Nothing for the pilot — this is genuinely free for the first 3 hotels. After the pilot, we're planning €199/month. But that conversation is 6 weeks away and depends on whether you actually find it useful."

## 8. Tracking your 3 pilots

Keep this simple — a spreadsheet with 8 columns is enough.

| Hotel | GM name | Source | Date contacted | Status | Last touch | Pilot start | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | | | | |

Status values: `found` → `contacted` → `replied` → `demo booked` → `demo done` → `onboarding` → `active pilot`

**Target funnel to get 3 pilots:**

You need roughly:
- 30 hotels identified
- 20 contacted
- 8–10 who reply
- 5–6 who take a demo call
- 3 who onboard

That's a 10% contact-to-pilot conversion rate — realistic for a warm, personalised outreach to a specific ICP. Cold spray-and-pray gets 1–2%.

**Timeline:** at 5 outreaches per day, you can contact 20 hotels in 4 days. Expect 3 weeks from first contact to first active pilot (demo scheduling, connection calls, etc.).

## 9. What success looks like at week 6

- 3 hotels have been using Fonda daily for 2+ weeks
- You've had at least one "I showed this to my owner and she loved it" moment
- You have specific feedback on what the briefing gets wrong or misses
- At least one pilot says yes to paying when you ask

If that's where you are at week 6, everything after — building, raising, growing — becomes dramatically easier to do and easier to justify.
